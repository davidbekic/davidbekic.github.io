    David Bekic

"nets", derived from the plural of the ancient greek word "net", is a study of the total ubiquity of nets. This study reflects on the duality of nets, oscillating between the fixed and the set loose, the complacent and the unrigid, the strung and unstrung as well as the woven and the unwoven.

Each net can be said to exist fully as an individual. However, it would be no less true to proclaim it as a group being composed of many individual strings, vertices and particles.

By studying the transition, or lack thereof, from the net to network, from nets to networks and ultimately from net to networks of nets, we find many answers and analogies to a plethora of biological, digital, anthropological and psychological phenomena.
